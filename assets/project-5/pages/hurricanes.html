Hurricanes

The Hurricanes were founded in 1971 as the New England Whalers in the WHA. The Whalers had a lot of success after starting their franchise, winning the East division in the WHA in their first three seasons. They competed for the World Trophy in 1978 but lost to the Winnipeg Jets. They joined the NHL in 1979 as a part of the NHL-WHA merger, renaming themselves to the Hartford Whalers. In 1997, they relocated to Raleigh, North Carolina and became the Carolina Hurricanes. In 2002, they made the Stanley Cup Final but lost to the Red Wings in five games. In 2006, they made the Stanley Cup Final again and beat the Oilers in seven games, winning their first Stanley Cup and championship in their entire history.
