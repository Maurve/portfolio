Devils

The Devils were founded as the Kansas City Scouts in 1974 along with the Capitals. The Scouts moved to Denver in 1976 and became the Colorado Rockies (now an MLB team). In 1982, they moved to New Jersey and have been there ever since. They play at Prudential Center. In 1987, the Devils hired Lou Lamoriello. During his years as general manager, the Devils made the playoffs all but three times between 1988 and 2012. They made the playoffs 13 seasons in a row from 1997 to 2010. They won the Atlantic Division 9 times until the NHL realinged divisions in 2013. The Devils have won three Stanley Cups. They won in 1995, 2000, and 2003. They lost in the Stanley Cup Final in 2001 and 2012.
