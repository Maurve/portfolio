Capitals

The Capitals were founded in 1974 as an expansion team along with the Kansas City Scouts. In their first year, they suffered the worst season in NHL history, going 8-67-5 in the 1974-75 season. In 1982, David Poile was hired as general manager, and he helped turned the Capitals franchise around. The Capitals became a regular playoff contender for the 14 following seasons but never won a Stanley Cup during those years. The Capitals won their first Presidents' Trophy in franchise history during the 2009-10 season but lost in the conferece quarterfinals to the Canadiens. They won the Presidents' Trophy in both the 2015-16 and 2016-17 seasons but didn't win the Stanley Cup. They finally won it all in 2017-18, defeating the then-expansion Golden Knights in the Stanley Cup Final 4-1. Ovechkin won his first Stanley Cup.
